Muhammad Ali Jinnah

(Founder of our nation)

Muhammad Ali Jinnah often referred to as the"Father of the Nation" in pakistan,was Prominent political leader and the founder of pakistan.



Here are some key details about his life and contributions:

DATE OF BIRTH

Muhammad Ali Jinnah was born on 25,December,1876

Early Life and Education:

Jinnah born into a merchant family of Gujarati descent. He received his early education in Karachi and later attended school in Bombay (now Mumbai). He went to London in 1892 study law at Lincoln's Inn and was called to the Bar in1896.

Entry into politics

Jinnah began his political career in 1906 when he joined the indian Nation congress(INC)and initailly advocated for Hindu_Muslim unity in india He was a member of the INCfor severl years and worked closely with leader like Mahatma Gandhi and jawaharlal Nehru in the early 20thcentury.

Shift to the All-India Muslim League:

In the1920s, Jinnah became increasingly disillusioned with the INC's approach and its lack of focus on the concerns of Muslims. In 1913, he joined the All-India Muslim League, where he would eventually rise to prominence as a key advocate for Muslim rights in a Hindu-majority India. By the1930s, Jinnah started calling for a separate nation for Muslims to protect their political, social, and economic rights. This led to the formation of Pakistan.

Creation of Pakistan:

In1940, Jinnah presented the Lahore Resolution, calling for the creation of a separate Muslim state. This was a turning point in the Indian independence movement. Over the next seven years, Jinnah and the Muslim League worked tirelessly to achieve the goal of a separate nation, which ultimately led to the creation of Pakistan on August 1947, the partition of British India. Jinnah became the first Governor-General of Pakistan and played a key role in shaping the new country.

Leadership and Vision for Pakistan:

As the leader of Pakistan, Jinnah was focused on building a democratic and inclusive society, where religious minorities would have their rights protected. He famously emphasized that Pakistan would be a state where people would have freedom of worship, stating,"You are free to go to your temples, you are free to go to your mosques or to any other place of worship in this State of Pakistan." Jinnah worked to establish Pakistan's governmental institutions and laid the foundation for the country's constitution.

Death and Legacy:

Jinnah's health declined rapidly in the months following the creation of Pakistan. He died on September 11, 1948, just over a year after the formation of the country. His legacy remains influential in Pakistan today. He is remembered as the"Quaid-e-Azam," visionary leader who secured a homeland for Muslims in the Indian subcontinent.

MAZAR-E-QUAID:

Mazar-e--Quaid also known as the Jinnah Mausoleum, is a national monument located in Karachi, Pakistan. It is the final resting place of Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah, the founder of Pakistan. Mazar-e-Quaid was built in 1970 to honor the memory of Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah and his sister, Fatima Jinnah.

"Mazar-e-Quaid is a place where the people of Pakistan commemorate their Quaid-e-Azam and recall his struggle for independence."
